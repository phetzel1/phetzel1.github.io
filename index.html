Projects

One of the beneficial aspects of living in the present is the abundance of open data. In particular, I typically set my sights on government-provided open data because:

  1. There's a lot of it.

  2. It's usually messy.

I use these data sets to both learn more about how to shape/visualize data and because I have a genuine interest for money in politics.

Federal Election Commission Data

In this project I am attempting to solve three different questions through public FEC filings:

  • Which companies give the most money over time?
  • Which political direction companies are likely to donate?
  • Which candidates are receiving the most money over time?

The data comes in pipe-delimited files and is updated frequently.

Lobbying Disclosure Data

By law, lobbying disclosures must be made on a quarterly basis. I have two questions for this project:

  • What entities lobby the hardest?
  • What issues are lobbied the hardest?

The data comes in XML format, which has allowed me to become familiar with the XPath querying language.
